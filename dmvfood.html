Editor's Picks

Three spots earning repeat visits this month across the district.

Georgetown, DC
Japanese Omakase · Sushi Cafe

Two Nine Omakase

Tucked down an alleyway off Wisconsin Ave, Two Nine runs a daytime chirashi cafe with a kaiseki-inspired omakase counter upstairs. Chef Zach Ramos sources much of the seafood straight from Toyosu Market in Tokyo, and the nigiri menu changes daily. Ring the bell — they'll walk you up.

Downtown, DC
Michelin-Recognized · Latin American

Seven Reasons

Chef Enrique Limardo's flagship pulls from Venezuela, Peru, and the Caribbean, plating dishes as bold in color as they are in flavor. The patacón piña colada and the cacao-basil crumble dessert are the two hardest things on the menu to skip.

4.5 Read the write-up →
Rockville, MD
Korean BBQ · Premium Cuts

Chadol Korean BBQ

The original Chadol location, and still the standard-bearer — Certified Angus beef and top-2%-grade Duroc pork cooked tableside on built-in grills. The brisket and galbi are the two orders regulars won't let you skip.
